The Unexpected gift

June 7, 2025

#Gear #Equipment #Birthday

God always has a way of doing things. My journey into photography took an unexpected turn this year when I received a birthday scare that would completely shift my perspective on my health.

The Unexpected Gift

As my birthday approached, I was eager to celebrate, I usually don't get to take the time to celebrate myself, I usually find happiness in working, or investing in myself/ my future. This year, being in a new location, I wanted to make the most of it. But, god had other plans, 3 days before my birthday, I felt a lot of chest tightness, so much so that I couldn't focus on my work, I decided to go to urgent care, and after a few tests, I was told that I was having symptoms of a heart attack. The news hit me like a ton of bricks. I had always been active, always taken care of myself, and now I was facing something that could change my life forever. I wasn't panicked, until I was rushed to a hospital in an ambulance. I was a little nervous, but I said to myself, "no way im about to have a heart attack, I'm invincible." As I get to the hospital, I was brought to an operating room, and I was told that they were about to go into my heart to check for any blockages. Then panic settled in.

After a heart catheterization, I woke up. I was still high from the anesthesia, but I could vividly remember the doctors and surgeons faintly singing happy birthday. I can't remember much, but I do remember the comfort provided by the doctors and surgeons during my procedure. During the next few hours I found myself truly reflecting from how traumatizing and how close I was to... death.

Recovery

After the procedure, I was told that I was totally fine. I had no blockages, no issues, and that I was in great health. The doctors explained that the symptoms I experienced were due to high blood pressure, stress, and anxiety. I was relieved, but also shaken. My parents were still on their way, catching the first thing smoking in the morning, but, by god's grace, my brother and Aunt were there for me when I woke up. This whole thing, it was a wake-up call that I needed to take better care of myself, both physically and mentally.

Camera Body

While the camera body gets most of the attention, it's important to remember that it's just one part of the equation. That said, having a reliable, capable camera that you understand inside and out is crucial. Modern mirrorless cameras offer incredible capabilities in compact packages, making them perfect for both beginners and professionals.

The Right Lenses

If I've learned anything in my photography journey, it's that lenses make a bigger difference than most people realize. A versatile lens can open up creative possibilities you never knew existed. The birthday gift I received was actually a lens that I had been researching for months – one that would allow me to explore different focal lengths and discover new perspectives.

Support Equipment

Often overlooked but absolutely essential: a good tripod, extra batteries, memory cards, and a reliable camera bag. These aren't the glamorous purchases, but they're the ones that ensure you never miss a shot due to dead batteries or lack of storage space.

Quality Over Quantity

One lesson I've learned is that it's better to have fewer pieces of high-quality equipment than a bag full of mediocre gear. Every item in your kit should serve a purpose and be something you reach for regularly. This birthday gift reinforced that philosophy – it wasn't about having the latest and greatest, but about having the right tool for my specific needs and style.

The Investment Mindset

Photography gear can be expensive, and as a student, every purchase requires careful consideration. But I've come to view quality equipment as an investment in my craft. Good gear, properly maintained, will last for years and help you create images you'll be proud of for a lifetime.

The unexpected birthday present taught me that sometimes investments in our passions come from the most unexpected places. The support from family and friends who believe in your dreams is just as valuable as any piece of equipment.

Looking Ahead

I know that from now on I must prioritize my health, the stress, has to be gone, I have to take care of myself, and I have to be more intentional about how I approach my photography. This birthday gift wasn't just a new lens; it was a reminder that life is precious, and every moment, could be your last.

Julius L. Jones Jr.

Student, photographer, and storyteller capturing moments that matter.
