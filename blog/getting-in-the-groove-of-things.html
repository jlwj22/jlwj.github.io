Getting in the Groove of Things

February 20, 2024

#Photography #Learning #Growth

After a few days of picking up my camera sporadically, I THINK I'm finally starting to find my rhythm. There's something different about this phase of my photography journey – it feels less forced, more natural.

Finding My Flow

The breakthrough came when I stopped overthinking every shot. Instead of analyzing compositions for minutes, I started trusting my instincts. Walking through the city with my camera now feels like a conversation rather than an exam.

I've been shooting daily for three weeks now, and the muscle memory is building. Camera settings that once required conscious thought now happen automatically. My eye is getting quicker at spotting moments worth capturing.

What Changed

The shift happened gradually, then all at once. A few key things clicked:

  • Consistency: Shooting every day, even if just for 20 minutes
  • Simplicity: Focusing on one subject or style at a time
  • Patience: Waiting for the right moment instead of forcing it
  • Trust: Believing in my eye and instincts

The Sweet Spot

There's this sweet spot I've discovered – when technical knowledge becomes second nature and creativity can flow freely. It's like learning to drive; once you stop thinking about the mechanics, you can focus on the journey.

My recent street photography sessions have felt different. I'm more present, more aware of the subtle moments that make a scene compelling. The camera feels like an extension of my vision rather than a tool I'm wielding.

What's Next

Now that I've found this groove, I want to push further. I'm planning to explore new neighborhoods, experiment with different lighting conditions, and maybe even try some night photography.

The goal isn't to maintain this exact rhythm forever – growth means evolving. But having this foundation of confidence and flow gives me a solid base to build from.

Julius L. Jones Jr.

Student, photographer, and storyteller capturing moments that matter.
