The Start of My Journey

June 2, 2025

#Photography #Journey #Hobby

Every photographer has a story about how they began their journey into the world of capturing moments. For me, it wasn't a sudden revelation or a childhood dream that slowly manifested into reality. Instead, it was a long-awaited investment into something I had been curious about for years.

Growing up in a family of photographers, I was always surrounded by cameras, lenses, and stories of the perfect shot. My father would often show me his collection of vintage cameras, each one holding memories of different eras and adventures. But despite this exposure, I never felt the pull to pick up a camera myself – until recently.

The Investment

The decision to finally invest in my own photography equipment wasn't taken lightly. As a student at Morehouse College pursuing Computer Science with a minor in Data Science and Analytics, every dollar counts. But there comes a moment when you realize that some investments aren't just about money – they're about investing in yourself and your passions.

After months of research, reading reviews, and saving up, I finally made the purchase that would change my perspective on the world around me. The weight of the camera in my hands for the first time was both exciting and intimidating. This wasn't just a device; it was a tool that would allow me to capture the fleeting moments that make life beautiful.

First Steps

My first few weeks with the camera were humbling. Despite growing up around photographers, I quickly realized that understanding photography and actually doing it are two completely different things. The technical aspects – understanding aperture, shutter speed, ISO – were just the beginning. The real challenge was learning to see the world through a different lens, quite literally.

I started small, photographing everyday objects around my dorm room, the campus during different times of day, and candid moments with friends. Each shot was a learning experience, and each mistake taught me something new about both the technical and artistic sides of photography.

Finding My Voice

What struck me most during those early days was how photography forced me to slow down and really observe my surroundings. In our fast-paced world, especially as a busy college student, it's easy to rush through life without truly seeing what's around us. Photography changed that for me.

I began to notice the way light falls across the buildings on campus during golden hour, the expressions on people's faces during moments of genuine laughter, and the intricate patterns in nature that I had previously overlooked. Photography wasn't just about capturing images; it was about training my eye to see beauty in the ordinary.

The Journey Continues

Looking back at those first few months of my photography journey, I'm amazed at how much I've grown, not just as a photographer, but as a person. This hobby – this passion – has taught me patience, persistence, and the importance of perspective.

Every photograph I take now is a step forward in this journey. Some shots are successful, others serve as learning experiences, but each one contributes to my growth as an artist. The investment I made wasn't just in equipment; it was in a new way of seeing and experiencing the world.

As I continue to develop my skills and find my unique voice in photography, I'm excited about what lies ahead. This is just the beginning of what I hope will be a lifelong journey of capturing moments, telling stories, and sharing the beauty I see through my lens with others.

Thank you for joining me on this journey. I hope my story inspires you to invest in your own passions, whatever they may be.

Julius L. Jones Jr.

Student, photographer, and storyteller capturing moments that matter.
